How are you helping?

Track and understand the spread of the virus

Efforts in coordinating international research that will ensure a transparent process that engages a broad group.

A network of specialised referral laboratories is activated and supporting national laboratories.

Partners working together to strengthen global diagnostic capacity to improve surveillance and track the spread of disease.

Ensure patients get the care they need

Clinicians caring for Covid-19 patients during all phases of their disease.

Operating chest imaging according to different circumstances in acute care of adult patients.

Provision of Toolkit to low or middle income countries to help manage patients with acute respiratory infection.

Equip frontline workers with essential supplies and information

Ensure all countries and populations in need are in touch with crucial supplies and commodities.

The Pandemic Supply Chain Network is activated to ascertain the availability of crucial supplies.

Gather information from global network of partners from affected countries and professional global partners.

Accelerate efforts to develop vaccines, tests, and treatments

It is important to evaluate as many vaccines as possible as we cannot predict how many will turn out to be viable.

Efforts on ensuring the availability of tests, including screening validation of current academic and commercially available.

Establishment of The Solidarity Trial aims to discover any drugs slowing the progression of the disease or improving survival rates.
